Read the rest of Section 15.3 of Applied Discrete Structures. The family \(S_n\) of symmetric groups is extremely important in group theory, particularly when it comes to doing computations. There is a theorem called Cayley's Theorem that essentially says that every group is subgroup of some symmetric group. This means that if you intend to do group computations, it's natural to implement the work in terms of permutations.
