Item 8.2.2.c.

No, the inverse of a postitive integer \(a\) would be \(b\) such that \(a \square b=1\text{.}\) Therefore \(a+b-1=1\text{,}\) or \(b=-a+2\text{.}\) But since we assume \(a\) is a positive integer \(b\) is not positive unless \(a = 1\text{,}\) and so only 1 have an inverse.
