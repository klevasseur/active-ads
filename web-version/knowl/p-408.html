Interest is earned on investments by adding to the invested amount, called the principle. An interest rate is a percentage of the principle that is earned. For example if you invest $2,000 in an investment that earns 3 percent, your interest in one year would be \(\$2,000\cdot 0.03 =\$60\text{.}\) This is added to principle. The new principle is normally computed in one step by multiplying by \(1.03\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \$2,000+ \$2,000\cdot 0.03 =\$2,000\cdot 1.03 = \$2,060. \end{equation*}
