The chromatic polynomial of a graph, \(G\) is a polynomial \(g(x)\) such that for each positive integer \(n\text{,}\) \(g(n)\) equals the number of different ways you can color \(G\) with \(n\) colors. For example, the chromatic polynomial of a \(K_3\) is \(x(x-1)(x-2)\text{.}\)
