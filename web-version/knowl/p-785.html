There are advantages to cycle notation. One is that the order of an element can be determined by its cycle structure.

  1. What are the orders of the following permutations?

    1. \(\displaystyle (1,2,3)\)

    2. \(\displaystyle (1,2,3,4)\)

    3. \(\displaystyle (1,2,3)(4,5)\)

    4. \(\displaystyle (1,2,3)(4,5,6)\)

    5. \(\displaystyle (1,2,3,4)(5,6)\)

    6. \(\displaystyle (1,6)(1,5)(1,4)(1,3)(1,2)\)

  2. Based on the result in the previous problem, predict the orders of the following permutations without actually computing their powers.

    1. \(\displaystyle (1,2,3,4,5,6,7)\)

    2. \(\displaystyle (1,2,3,4)(5,6,7,8)\)

    3. \(\displaystyle (1,2,3,4)(5,6,7)\)

  3. Among all permutations in \(S_{10}\text{,}\) what is their largest order? How about \(S_{11}\text{?}\)

  4. Prove that if \(\sigma\) and \(\phi\) are permutations, then the order of \(\phi \circ \sigma \circ \phi^{-1}\) is equal to the order of \(\sigma\text{.}\)
