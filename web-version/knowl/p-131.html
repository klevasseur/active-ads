Consider the statement “If Boris visits Hampton Beach, then he eats fried clams.”

  1. Write the converse of the statement.

  2. Write the contrapositive of the statement.

  3. Is it possible for the contrapositive to be false? If it was, what would that tell you?

  4. Suppose the original statement is true, and that Boris eats fried clams. Can you conclude anything (about his travels)?

  5. Suppose the original statement is true, and that Boris does not eat fried clams. Can you conclude anything (about his travels)?
