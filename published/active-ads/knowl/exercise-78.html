Exercise 2.
If \(A\) is a an \(m \times n\) matrix, we define the transpose of A to be the \(n \times m\) matrix whose rows are the columns of \(A\text{.}\) For example, the transpose of
\begin{equation*} \left( \begin{array}{ccc} 1 &2 &3 \\ 4 &5 &6 \\ \end{array} \right) \textrm{ is } \left( \begin{array}{cc} 1 &4 \\ 2 &5 \\ 3 &6 \\ \end{array} \right). \end{equation*}
The notation \(A^t\) is used for the transpose of \(A\text{.}\)
  1. If \(A\) is an \(m \times n\) matrix, are the products \(A A^t\) and \(A^t A \) defined? What are the orders of the products that are defined?
  2. Given the following matrix, what useful information might you get from the products \(A A^t\) or \(A^t A\text{.?}\)
    \begin{equation*} A=\left( \begin{array}{ccccc} 16 &11 &4 &3 &15 \\ 16 &17 &13 &12 &6 \\ \end{array} \right) \end{equation*}
